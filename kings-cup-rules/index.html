Kings Cup rules & card meanings

Every card explained at a glance — the common US meanings and the UK versions side by side, so your table can stop arguing and start playing.

The game in one paragraph: Kings Cup (also called King's Cup, Ring of Fire, or Circle of Death) is the classic card-drawing drinking game. A full deck sits face-down in a circle around a central cup. Players take turns drawing; every rank means a rule. The first three Kings each add a splash of drink to the middle cup — whoever draws the fourth King drinks the whole thing, and that's the game.

The rules that need a sentence more

Waterfall (Ace)

Everyone starts drinking at the same time. The drawer stops whenever they like; each player can only stop once the person before them has stopped. Last in the chain suffers most — that's the point.

Mate (8)

Pick another player. From now on, whenever one of you drinks, you both drink. In the UK version the bond breaks at the next 8; many US tables keep it for the whole game. Agree which before you start.

Never Have I Ever (Jack, US version)

Everyone holds up three fingers. Go around saying things you've never done; anyone who has done it drops a finger. First to lose all three drinks. Need material? We keep 50 Never Have I Ever questions ready to go.

Question Master (Queen)

Until the next Queen, anyone who answers a question the Question Master asks must drink. Answering is a reflex — this rule prints drinks.

The Kings

Kings 1–3: pour a splash of whatever you're drinking into the middle cup. King 4: the drawer downs the cup — a mixture nobody designed and nobody would order — and the game ends.

Setup, quickly

  1. Empty cup in the middle of the table.
  2. Full 52-card deck face-down in a ring around it.
  3. 3+ players, drinks in hand, draw clockwise, one card per turn.

Kings Cup FAQ

What does the 4 card mean in Kings Cup?

Depends where you learned it: in the common US version 4 is "floor" — last player to touch the floor drinks. In the UK version the 4 rhyme targets the girls. If your table has both camps, pick one before the game starts.

What does the Jack mean?

US tables usually play Jack as a quick Never Have I Ever round; UK tables play it as Rule Maker — the drawer invents a rule that stands until the next Jack, and rule-breakers drink.

When does Kings Cup end?

When the fourth King is drawn and its drawer downs the middle cup. Some groups play out the remaining ring, but the fourth King is the traditional full-time whistle.

Is Kings Cup the same as Ring of Fire?

Yes — same game, different name. Kings Cup (or King's Cup) is the usual US name; Ring of Fire is the UK one; Circle of Death also gets used. The card meanings vary a little between them, which is exactly what the table above is for.

Can you play Kings Cup without alcohol?

Yes — swap sips for forfeits (the Waterfall becomes a group squat hold, the Kings Cup becomes performing a group-built dance routine). The Ring of Fire guide lists a sober version for all 13 cards.

Play it right: Kings Cup is an 18+ drinking game. Sips, not chugs — and nobody has to drink who doesn't want to; that's what forfeit versions are for. Look after your mates.
