Most Likely To: 40 questions for maximum pointing

The simplest party game there is — and the most personal. Here are the rules in one line and 40 questions written for groups who actually know each other's business.

How to play: read a "who's most likely to…" out loud. Count down from three, and everyone points at someone — no abstaining, self-votes allowed if you're honest. Whoever has the most fingers on them drinks (or takes a forfeit sober). Split decision? Both drink, then the room debates it.

Why this game works (and when it doesn't)

Most Likely To runs on shared history — the pointing is the punchline, and the defence speeches are the content. It's the best game on this site for a group that's known each other for years, and the weakest for a room of strangers (they've got nothing to point with — give them Never Have I Ever first and come back in an hour).

Most Likely To FAQ

What are the rules of Most Likely To?

Someone reads a "who's most likely to…" question, everyone simultaneously points at a player on the count of three, and whoever collects the most fingers drinks or takes a forfeit. No abstaining — pointing is mandatory.

What happens on a tie?

Both drink. Then the room argues about who deserved it more, which is the game working as intended.

Can you vote for yourself?

Yes — a confident self-point is one of the great moves in the game. It counts like any other finger.

Play it right: 18+ for the drinking version, and read the room — the questions are about affectionate exposure, not actual grievances. Look after your mates.
