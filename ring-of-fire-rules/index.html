Ring of Fire rules: every card explained

The definitive UK rules — setup, all 13 card meanings from Waterfall to King's Cup, the house rules worth arguing about, and a sober version of every single card.

The 30-second version: spread a full deck face-down in a ring around a cup. Take turns drawing a card — every rank has a rule (Ace = Waterfall, 2 = You, 3 = Me…). Do the rule, next player draws. Each King adds a splash of drink to the middle cup; whoever draws the fourth King downs the lot, and the game ends.

You need: 3+ players, a deck of cards, drinks, one cup for the middle. About 30–60 minutes.

Setup

  1. Put an empty cup in the middle of the table. This is the King's Cup — nobody touches it until a King says so.
  2. Spread all 52 cards face-down in a circle around the cup. That circle is the ring of fire.
  3. Sit everyone around the table with a drink. Pick someone to go first (loser of the last game, birthday person, whoever's late — your call).
  4. Going clockwise, each player draws one card per turn, shows the group, and everyone does what the card says.

That's it. The whole game is in the card meanings below — which is also where every argument starts, so bookmark this page and settle them properly.

The cards in detail

Ace — Waterfall

Everyone starts drinking at once. You stop whenever you like — but nobody else can stop until the person before them does.

The person who drew the Ace controls everyone's fate: if they keep going, the whole circle keeps going. Sit next to someone merciful.

SOBER VERSION Everyone holds a squat at once. You stand whenever you like — but nobody else can stand until the person before them does.

2 — You

Point at a player. They take a sip.

SOBER VERSION Point at a player. They take a forfeit.

3 — Me

Unlucky — that one's on you. Take a sip.

SOBER VERSION Unlucky — that one's on you. Take a forfeit.

4 — Whores

Girls, that's you — every girl takes a sip.

Yes, that's really what it's called. The rhyme is the rule: four — whores. Some groups play it as "floor" (last to touch the floor drinks) if they'd rather keep it family-friendly-ish.

SOBER VERSION Girls, that's you — every girl takes a forfeit.

5 — Thumbmaster

You're the Thumbmaster until the next 5. Sneak your thumb onto the table at any moment — last player to copy you drinks.

The art is patience. Wait until an argument breaks out or someone's telling a story, then slide the thumb on quietly. Chaos.

SOBER VERSION You're the Thumbmaster until the next 5. Sneak your thumb onto the table at any moment — last player to copy you takes a forfeit.

6 — Dicks

Lads, that's you — every guy takes a sip.

Same rhyme logic: six — dicks. Nobody said the game was sophisticated.

SOBER VERSION Lads, that's you — every guy takes a forfeit.

7 — Heaven

Reach for the sky! Last player to raise a hand takes a sip.

Like Thumbmaster, this rewards the player who does it dead casually, mid-sentence, while everyone's distracted.

SOBER VERSION Reach for the sky! Last player to raise a hand takes a forfeit.

8 — Mate

Choose a mate. Until the next 8 is drawn, whenever one of you drinks, you both drink.

Choosing the person who's drinking the most is a declaration of war on yourself. Choose wisely.

SOBER VERSION Choose a mate. Until the next 8 is drawn, whenever one of you gets a forfeit, you both do it.

9 — Rhyme

Say a word. Go around the circle rhyming with it. First to hesitate or repeat takes a sip.

Pro move: start with "orange" or "purple" and watch the next player suffer. (House-rule whether that's legal.)

SOBER VERSION Say a word. Go around the circle rhyming with it. First to hesitate or repeat takes a forfeit.

10 — Categories

Pick a category — cereals, footballers, things in a pencil case. Go around naming them. First to blank takes a sip.

The best categories are specific enough to run dry: "Premier League goalkeepers", not "animals".

SOBER VERSION Pick a category — cereals, footballers, things in a pencil case. Go around naming them. First to blank takes a forfeit.

Jack — Rule Maker

Invent a rule (no first names, left hand only…). It stands until the next Jack. Rule-breakers drink.

Classics: no saying "drink", no pointing, everyone speaks in an accent, little green man (tap an invisible man off your can before you sip). Rules stack — by the fourth Jack, remembering the rules is the game.

SOBER VERSION Invent a rule (no first names, no saying "yes"…). It stands until the next Jack. Rule-breakers take a forfeit.

Queen — Question Master

You're the Question Master until the next Queen. Anyone who answers a question you ask must take a sip.

The trap is that answering is a reflex. "What time is it?" catches someone every single game.

SOBER VERSION You're the Question Master until the next Queen. Anyone who answers a question you ask takes a forfeit.

King — King's Cup

Pour a splash of your drink into the cup in the middle. Whoever draws the FOURTH King downs the lot.

Three Kings drawn is maximum tension — every card after that could be the one. The mixture in that cup is legally your problem.

SOBER VERSION Add one move to the group dance routine. Whoever draws the FOURTH King performs the whole routine, start to finish.

How the game ends

The fourth King ends the game — its drawer downs the King's Cup and everyone else feels enormously relieved. Some groups keep drawing until the ring is done; most start a new round, because the setup takes ninety seconds and someone always demands revenge.

House rules worth knowing

  • Break the circle, down your drink. If drawing a card breaks the ring (leaves a gap so it's no longer a connected circle), the breaker downs their drink. The single most common house rule in the UK.
  • 4 = floor. The polite variant: last player to touch the floor drinks, instead of the girls-drink rule.
  • Snake eyes. One player is chosen; anyone who makes eye contact with them drinks. Usually arrives via a Jack.
  • Toilet card. No leaving the table until you've drawn a specific rank — usually played with any 10. Brutal. Agree it before the game, not during.

Whatever you pick: agree house rules before the first card. Ring of Fire arguments have ended friendships (temporarily).

Playing it sober

Every card above has a forfeit version, and the game genuinely holds up — Waterfall as a group squat is arguably crueller than the drinking version. Mixed groups can run both at once: drinkers sip, everyone else takes the forfeit. Nobody sits out and nobody has to explain themselves.

Ring of Fire FAQ

Is Ring of Fire the same as Kings Cup?

Yes — Ring of Fire, Kings Cup, King's Cup and Circle of Death are the same game. "Ring of Fire" is the usual name in the UK; "Kings Cup" is more common in the US. Card meanings vary slightly by region and by house rules, which is what the arguments are about.

How many people do you need?

Three is the minimum for it to work, five to eight is the sweet spot. Big groups just mean more time between your turns — the group cards (Waterfall, Heaven, Categories) still hit everyone.

What happens when you draw a King?

You pour a splash of your drink into the cup in the middle. The first three Kings build the mixture; whoever draws the fourth King downs the whole cup and the game ends.

Can you play Ring of Fire without alcohol?

Yes — swap every sip for a forfeit (5 jumping jacks, speak in an accent for two rounds, and so on). Every card meaning above includes its sober version. The Antics app has a Sober mode that does this swap automatically.

What order do you deal the cards?

You don't deal — all 52 cards go face-down in a ring around the middle cup, and players draw one at a time. If a card leaves the ring with a gap, see the "break the circle" house rule above.

Play it right: Ring of Fire is an 18+ drinking game. Sip, don't neck — a Waterfall works with lemonade and the game is funnier when everyone remembers it. Look after your mates, and never pressure anyone to drink: that's what the forfeit rules are for.
